Winter Home Prep: Your Complete NZ Checklist

Auckland winters aren't brutal by global standards, but they're wetter and windier than most homeowners plan for. A bit of preventative work in April and May can save you thousands in repairs and a lot of misery come July. Here's the checklist we run through with every PeakHome client heading into the cooler months.

1. Clear and check your gutters

This is the single most important winter prep job and the one most homeowners skip. Blocked gutters overflow into your eaves, soffits, and wall cavities — causing rot and moisture damage that can take months to appear but thousands of dollars to fix.

Clear all leaf litter and debris from gutters and downpipes before the heavy rain season starts. While you're up there, check for sagging sections, rust spots, and joints that have pulled apart. A full gutter clean and inspection for an average Auckland home costs around $150–$250 — well worth it.

2. Service your hot water cylinder

Cold showers are bad enough in summer. In July, a failed hot water cylinder is a genuine emergency. Most NZ homes have a mains-pressure or low-pressure cylinder that should be inspected every 3–5 years. Have a licensed plumber check the anode rod, the temperature pressure relief (TPR) valve, and the element if it's electric. Replacing an anode rod costs around $80 — replacing an entire cylinder because one failed costs $1,500 or more.

If your cylinder is more than 15 years old and hasn't been serviced, budget for a replacement rather than waiting for it to let go.

3. Check your roof for loose or lifted iron

Auckland's winter storms come with serious wind. Corrugated iron roofing that looked fine during summer can lift or crack under gale-force gusts, turning a minor maintenance job into a full emergency repair in the middle of a rainstorm. Get a roofer to do a visual check — from ground level if nothing looks obviously wrong, or a full inspection if the roof is older than 20 years or you've had any interior water stains.

Pay particular attention to flashing around chimneys, skylights, and roof-to-wall junctions. These are where most roof leaks start.

4. Check insulation in the ceiling and underfloor

New Zealand homes are notoriously under-insulated. If your ceiling insulation is more than 15 years old, it may have compressed or shifted, losing much of its R-value. Underfloor insulation in older homes is often absent entirely. Both are relatively low-cost to install and will make a significant difference to your heating bills and comfort levels through winter.

EECA's Warmer Kiwi Homes programme offers subsidised insulation for eligible owner-occupiers — worth checking before booking any insulation work.

5. Service your heat pump

Heat pumps do most of their work in winter, and a dirty filter dramatically reduces efficiency. Clean your filters monthly during peak use, and have the unit professionally serviced every 2–3 years. A service includes a refrigerant check, coil cleaning, and electrical inspection — typically $150–$200 per unit.

6. Test smoke alarms and check CO detectors

Fires are more common in winter because people use heaters and open fireplaces they haven't touched in months. NZ law requires working smoke alarms in all residential properties. Test every alarm in your home and replace batteries. If you have a gas heater or log burner, install a carbon monoxide detector nearby.
